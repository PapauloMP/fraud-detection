# Datasets

This directory contains the textual *corpora* used for training, validating, and testing the multiclass classification model for detecting AI-assisted fraud and copy-typing. The *corpora* consist of **ENEM essays**, which are argumentative-expository texts employed as the textual domain for the experiments.

## Class Mapping

All final datasets are balanced and map the authorship spectrum into three distinct classes:
* **Class 0 (Human):** Authentic student essays. Extracted from the public dataset [Essay-BR](#references), prior to the popularization of LLMs (2015-2020).
* **Class 1 (Synthetic/AI):** Zero-shot generations produced by five high-performance LLMs (DeepSeek-V3.1, Qwen 3.5, GPT-OSS, Kimi 2.5, and Gemini 3 Flash), prompted under different proficiency-level personas.
* **Class 2 (Hybrid/Copy-Typing):** AI-generated texts subsequently subjected to controlled corruption via cross-model prompting.

## Training

These are the consolidated datasets used for the model's fine-tuning phase. Two versions were created with different noise thresholds and linguistic competence baselines.

### 1. `base_dataset.csv`
A reduced-scale dataset developed to serve as a control environment to evaluate the impact of sample size and noise threshold on model performance when compared against the extended version.
* **Total volume:** 1,285 essays.
* **Human:** 425 samples (only essays scoring **above 600 points**).
* **AI:** 430 samples.
* **Copy-Typing:** 430 samples.

### 2. `extended_dataset.csv`
Expanded dataset that maximizes linguistic diversity.
* **Total volume:** 3,693 essays.
* **Human:** 1,213 samples (essays scoring **above 440 points**).
* **AI:** 1,275 samples.
* **Copy-Typing:** 1,205 samples.

Both datasets share the same schema:
* `classe`: Target label (0, 1, or 2).
* `texto`: Essay text.
* `qtd_palavra`: Word count.
* `burstiness`: Burstiness metric (coefficient of variation of sentence lengths).
* `mtld`: Measure of Textual Lexical Diversity.
* `densidade_de_pontuacao`: Punctuation density.
* `qtd_virgula`: Comma count.
* `qtd_ponto_e_virgula`: Semicolon count.
* `qtd_ponto`: Period count.
* *Deprecated features:* `qtd_oov`, `taxa_oov` and `palavras_oov` (out-of-vocabulary metrics are no longer used).

## Evaluation and Testing

### 3. `test_dataset.csv`
Statistically independent and strictly isolated dataset for cross-validation and final evaluation. No human text, generated prompt, or copy fragment used in the training phase was reused here. 
* **Total volume:** 261 unseen essays.
* **Human:** 86 samples.
* **AI:** 90 samples.
* **Copy-Typing:** 85 samples.
* **Columns (Schema):**
  * `classe`: Target label (0, 1, or 2).
  * `texto`: Essay text.

## Intermediate Fragments

The files below are intermediate products of the data engineering pipeline. They are included in this repository to support empirical validation of the proposed methodology and testing of individual project scripts.

### 4. `ai_essays.csv`
Contains samples of synthetic essays generated by the five selected LLMs.
* **Columns (Schema):**
  * `tema`: Essay topic provided in the prompt.
  * `perfil_prompt`: Simulated student persona (proficiency level) used for generation.
  * `texto_ia`: AI-generated essay.
  * `fonte_llm`: LLM used for generation.

### 5. `cp_essays.csv`
Contains paired samples, with each original AI-generated essay presented alongside its corresponding post-corruption (copy-typing) version.
* **Columns (Schema):**
  * `tema`: Essay topic.
  * `perfil_prompt`: Simulated student persona used for the initial generation.
  * `texto_ia`: Original AI-generated essay.
  * `fonte_llm`: LLM used for the initial generation.
  * `texto_copy_typed`: Corrupted essay after applying the copy-typing simulation.
  * `copy_type_fonte_llm`: Secondary LLM used for cross-model corruption.
